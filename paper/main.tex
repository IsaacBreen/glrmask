\begin{abstract}
\todo{Write abstract}

We present a method for grammar-constrained decoding in large language models that achieves efficient inference through precomputation. Our approach compiles context-free grammars into deterministic weighted automata, enabling per-token constraint computation in time independent of the grammar size. We demonstrate significant speedups over existing methods while maintaining exact constraint satisfaction.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:introduction}

Large language models (LLMs) have demonstrated remarkable capabilities in generating text, code, and structured data. However, ensuring that generated outputs conform to specific syntactic constraints remains a challenge. Grammar-constrained decoding addresses this by restricting the model's output space to strings that satisfy a given grammar.
